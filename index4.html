This section explains the serious legal risks faced by those who placed their names on official HOA corporate filings prior to legal revitalization approval by the Florida Department of Economic Opportunity (DEO).

  • 📘 Florida Statute §817.155 – Falsification of Corporate Records
    Filing false officer information with Sunbiz (Division of Corporations) may be a third-degree felony.
  • 💼 Fraudulent Misrepresentation
    Using false information to access funds or transact on behalf of the HOA meets the elements of civil and criminal fraud.
  • 👤 Criminal Impersonation of Corporate Officer
    Claiming authority before DEO approval may be considered impersonation, especially if used to spend funds or authorize work.
  • 💰 Florida Statute §720.303(4) – Improper Use of Funds
    Spending money without proper legal authority prior to revitalization violates fiduciary duties.
  • 🏦 18 U.S. Code §1344 – Bank Fraud
    Accessing a financial account using a falsified Sunbiz record may constitute federal bank fraud.
  • 📉 Civil Liability – Conversion & Fiduciary Breach
    Controlling HOA assets without authority exposes parties to civil restitution and fiduciary breach claims.
